  • 20 votes: Plant is wilting
  • About a month ago we purchased some rosemary plants. After a few days the plants started to wilt. We transplanted to a larger container but the plants are still wilting. They are bonnie brand plants does this company have a problem because plants we bought last year did the same thing?

  • Answer: If the plants are wilting they may be getting too much water. Rosemary thrives in sunshine with soils on the slightly dry side. Water your plants as usual then wait a few days and stick your finger down into the soil. If it is still moist an inch below the soil surface you won't have to water for a few more days; if it feels dry, it's time to water. I think when you adjust your watering schedule your rosemary plants will perk up. Best wishes with your plants!
